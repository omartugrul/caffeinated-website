# Caffeinated -- Brand & Design Brief

> Feed this document into Google Stitch, Paper.design, or Replit to generate branding concepts and landing page layouts. This brief describes the business, audience, and emotional direction without prescribing specific colors, fonts, or visual details. Let the tools interpret and ideate.

---

## The Business

**Caffeinated** is a mobile espresso bar in Houston, Texas that caters corporate events, weddings, and private functions. We show up with a full espresso setup, make great drinks, and make whoever booked us look like a hero.

This is not a food truck. This is not a side hustle. Caffeinated operates like a modern, well-run company that happens to serve coffee. The brand should reflect that.

---

## The Customer

The primary customer is a **corporate office manager, executive assistant, or HR/People Ops person** in Houston. They're planning an office event, a team appreciation day, or a client meeting and they need coffee handled. They don't have time to vet ten vendors. They want to land on one website, feel confident immediately, and book in minutes.

Secondary customers are **couples planning weddings** and **event coordinators** booking for private functions.

---

## The Feeling

The brand should feel:

- **Confident** -- like a company that knows exactly what it's doing. No hedging, no "we're just getting started" energy.
- **Warm** -- approachable, not cold or sterile. Coffee is a warm, human experience. The brand should feel that way.
- **Clean** -- uncluttered, considered, intentional. Every element earns its place.
- **Modern** -- this could be a well-funded DTC brand or a polished SaaS company. Not a vintage coffee shop. Not rustic. Not handwritten chalk fonts.
- **Trustworthy** -- the office manager who books Caffeinated should feel zero anxiety about whether it will go well. The brand should radiate reliability.

The brand should NOT feel:

- Cute, whimsical, or quirky
- Rustic, vintage, or farmhouse
- Generic "coffee shop" (no coffee bean illustrations, no steam wisps, no latte art hero images)
- Cold, clinical, or overly corporate
- Cheap or discount-oriented

---

## Brand Reference

**Blank Street Coffee** is the closest reference point for the energy we want. Their brand is clean, modern, confident, and minimal. They feel like a tech company that serves coffee. Caffeinated should live in a similar space but with more warmth -- Blank Street can feel a bit cold. We want that same level of design quality with a warmer, bolder personality.

Other directional references (for energy, not direct copying):
- The confidence of a brand like **Glossier** or **Allbirds** -- they know their audience and don't try to be everything
- The warmth and approachability of **Sweetgreen** -- premium but never pretentious
- The clean digital presence of a modern SaaS landing page -- clear hierarchy, strong CTAs, lots of breathing room

---

## What Needs Designing

### 1. Landing Page Layout

Single-page marketing site. The page should flow through these sections:

1. **Hero** -- Bold headline with clear value proposition. One strong call-to-action. Imagery of the espresso cart in a corporate or event setting.
2. **How It Works** -- Three simple steps. Communicate that booking is effortless.
3. **Services/Packages** -- Three tiers displayed as cards. Approachable premium pricing.
4. **About** -- Brief, human, confident. Who we are and why we do this.
5. **Gallery** -- Grid of event photos showing the cart in different contexts (offices, outdoor events, weddings).
6. **Testimonials** -- Social proof from corporate clients.
7. **FAQ** -- Clean accordion or expandable list.
8. **Contact/Quote Form** -- Simple form. Name, email, event type, date, estimated guest count.
9. **Footer** -- Service area, social links, legal.

The page should feel spacious. Lots of white space. Strong typographic hierarchy. Not cramped or busy.

### 2. Logo Direction

The name "Caffeinated" is strong and should be the centerpiece. A bold, clean wordmark is the starting point. A subtle icon or mark can accompany it but isn't required. The logo needs to work across: the website, social media, printed cups, menu boards, and invoices.

### 3. Visual System

We need a cohesive visual system that includes: a color palette, typography pairing, button/CTA styles, card styles, and general component patterns. The system should feel consistent whether someone sees the website, a social media post, or a branded cup at an event.

---

## Imagery Direction

We'll be using AI-generated imagery to start. The imagery should feel:

- Warm, natural lighting (golden hour or soft indoor light)
- Lifestyle-oriented (people enjoying coffee at events, not product shots of cups)
- Contextual (show the cart in real settings: office lobbies, outdoor corporate events, wedding venues, rooftops)
- Diverse and authentic (Houston is one of the most diverse cities in the US)
- NOT stock photo energy. Should feel candid and real, even if generated.

---

## Tone of Voice

The copy on the website should sound like a smart, friendly professional -- not a corporate brochure and not a quirky Instagram caption. Direct, clear, warm. Short sentences. No jargon. The reader should immediately understand what Caffeinated does, who it's for, and how to book.

**Example headlines (directional, not final):**
- "Houston's Mobile Espresso Bar"
- "Coffee Catering, Handled."
- "Book a Barista in 60 Seconds"
- "Your Next Office Event, Elevated"
- "Great Coffee. Zero Hassle."

**Example body copy (directional):**
"Caffeinated is Houston's mobile espresso bar for corporate events, weddings, and private functions. We show up with everything -- the cart, the barista, the beans, the cups. You just pick a date."

---

## Technical Context

The website will be built in Next.js with Tailwind CSS and Framer Motion for animations. This is a static marketing page with no backend. The design should account for:

- Mobile-first layout (most visitors will be on phones)
- Smooth scroll navigation between sections
- Subtle, purposeful animations (not flashy)
- Fast load times (minimal heavy assets)

---

## What Success Looks Like

A corporate office manager in Houston googles "coffee catering Houston," lands on the Caffeinated site, and within 10 seconds thinks: "This looks legit. This looks easy. I'm going to book this." That's the bar.
